Our History

Hope for Recovery was developed out of a stirring I felt in 2019 to put together a 12-week bible-based recovery group. The group was to be real and relevant & designed to meet the practical and spiritual needs of recovery without being too religious. I had no idea where to begin, but God took me on a journey, which ended with the development of not just 1 x 12-week group but 6 x 12-week groups which we now run as a 2-year community-based recovery program.

— Craig Halkett

Structured Recovery Courses

A progressive journey of healing. Our program is built on six distinct, faith-based 12-week courses. Each course focuses on a different aspect, providing a clear pathway from initial hope to lasting freedom. Participants receive certificates for each course completed.

  • ⭐ 3 Rs for Recovery – Restoring broken relationships
  • ⭐ Holistic Health – Fruit of Freedom
  • ⭐ Overcoming Temptation – 3 S's for Sobriety

⏰ When: Mondays, 11am – 1:30pm

Key Work

1 to 1 support is available every Wednesday by appointment. A safe space to share burdens, discuss challenges and work through dedicated outcomes. Whatever your needs are we are here to help.

  • Recovery Planning – Track your recovery journey using our personal centre goals and outcome booklet
  • Advocacy support – Practical support for everyday challenges
  • Befriending – Someone you can trust and confide in.
  • Pastoral Counselling – Learn & apply biblical truths to receive healing from the past & hope for the future.

Secondary Schools Drug & Alcohol Awareness Program

Hope for Recovery within Secondary Schools

The HFR resources have been adapted to produce a ‘Drugs and Alcohol Awareness’ package for use within secondary schools with a target audience of S4/S5/S6 pupils.

The materials are delivered over 4 (50 minute) lessons with each lesson following the format below:

📖 Lesson Structure:

  • ‘This is my Story’ – A member of the HFR team with lived experience shares their personal story explaining: what led them into addiction; the impact addiction had on them; how they escaped from addiction; their hopes for the future.
  • Q&A Session – Pupils are invited to ask questions and discuss the team member’s ‘story’ with them.
  • Health and Wellbeing Workshop – Pupils are placed in small groups and supported by a team member to discuss and complete a worksheet on the relevant HWB topic. Pupils are encouraged to feedback their findings to the class before a summary of the main ‘take-away’ points is provided.

The HWB workshops covered over the 4 lessons are entitled:

🌱 Root Causes of Addiction 🧘 Stress Management 🙏 Gratitude is the Best Attitude 💪 Alternative Responses to Challenging Events
