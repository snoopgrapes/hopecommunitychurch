Your Involvement Can Transform Lives

Explore the meaningful ways you can support Hope Community Church and its Hope for Recovery ministry through prayer, giving, and volunteering.

Prayer is the foundational support for our mission to bring hope, healing, and wholeness to our community. Your prayers make a powerful difference.

  • Prayer Focuses: Please pray for our leaders, Pastors Craig and Yvonne, for wisdom and strength. Pray for participants in our recovery programs, for their healing and perseverance. Pray for our church and all our services, that they remain a source of practical support and transformation.
  • Prayer Updates: To pray with us regularly, subscribe to our weekly prayer updates. You will receive specific points to guide your intercession for the work happening here in Clydebank.

üí∑ Give

Your financial and material gifts directly sustain our life-changing work and allow us to offer services free of charge to those in need.

  • Financial Donations: We welcome one-off or recurring financial donations. Your support helps fund our 12-week recovery groups, community cafe, and emergency foodbank.
  • In-Kind Donations: We gratefully receive donations of food, furniture, and other goods. These items help us operate our services and can also be used to support our charitable activities.
  • Legacy Giving: Consider including Hope Community Church in your will. Your legacy will ensure that the mission of restoring broken lives continues for generations to come.
  • Support Our Work: Your generosity empowers us to provide a safe, nurturing environment and comprehensive support for individuals on their journey to recovery.
Make a Donation

ü§ù Volunteer

Offer your time and skills to be a direct part of someone's journey to freedom. We need mature Christians willing to come alongside those in need.

  • Direct Service Roles: Volunteer at our weekly Hope for Recovery groups as a facilitator or support helper, creating a safe and welcoming environment. Serve at our Community Cafe on Fridays, helping with food preparation, serving, or simply offering a friendly face and conversation.
  • Mentorship & Discipleship: Become a mentor or befriender. Walk one-on-one with someone in recovery, offering guidance and support as they rebuild their life.
  • Skilled Support: Offer your professional skills behind the scenes. We often need help with administration, fundraising, maintenance, and other specialized tasks that keep our ministries running.
  • Internships & Long-Term Service: We offer part-time and full-time internship opportunities for those seeking deeper ministry experience, combining practical service with theological training.
Volunteer With Us
