How do we help?

Residential Recovery

Rebuilding Hope, Healing and a sense of Home.
Our residences provide a nurturing environment where individuals can regain their sense of hope, experience healing from addiction and rebuild their lives. Through comprehensive support and guidance, we help them create a new foundation and a safe place to call home.

Strong Support System

Embracing a family culture.
We foster a culture of family and community, where individuals can experience the power of unity, unconditional love and renewal. Through shared activities, communal meals, worship and mutual support, we create a strong network that uplifts and encourages each person on their journey to recovery.

Empowering Futures

Unlocking Potential, Building Thriving Careers.
Our dedicated team works closely with individuals to identify their strengths, providing training, job experience and ongoing support. By unlocking their potential, we empower our residents to build thriving careers and long lasting change and stability.

Our Recovery Programs

Holistic Health

A 12-week course exploring physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual health and how they interconnect.

  • Spirit, soul & body connection
  • Emotional health & regulation
  • Mental health principles
  • Spiritual awakening

When: Mondays, 11am - 1:30pm

Fruit of Freedom

Based on the nine Fruit of the Spirit, this course helps replace negative behaviors with positive ones.

  • Love, joy, and peace
  • Patience and kindness
  • Goodness and faithfulness
  • Gentleness and self-control

Certificate: Available upon completion

Faith to Freedom Recovery Pathway

A structured 2-year journey to lasting transformation through our comprehensive recovery programs:

  1. Initial connection through Community Cafe or Sunday services
  2. 12-week courses (Fruit of Freedom & Holistic Health)
  3. Community integration through church life and small groups
  4. Leadership development opportunities

Each 12-week course can be taken individually or as part of the complete pathway

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm nervous about coming to a group for the first time. What should I expect? +

It's completely normal to feel nervous! When you arrive, you'll be warmly welcomed in a non-judgmental environment. Our groups begin with establishing group guidelines to ensure everyone feels safe and respected. You can participate as much or as little as you're comfortable with - there's no pressure to share until you're ready.

Do I need to be Christian to attend? +

No, our programs are open to everyone regardless of religious background or beliefs. While our approach is biblically-based, we welcome people from all faiths or no faith at all. Our primary goal is to provide support and practical tools for recovery in a compassionate environment.

What if I relapse during the program? +

Recovery is a journey with ups and downs. If you experience a setback, we encourage you to still come to the group. We offer grace and support, not judgment. Many of our facilitators have walked similar paths and understand the challenges of recovery. We'll help you learn from the experience and continue moving forward.
